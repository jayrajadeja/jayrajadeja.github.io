Bypassing browser thread limitations for complex mathematical rendering. A look into the performance delta between standard JS and WASM-compiled pipelines.

## The Problem

Complex mathematical visualizations in the browser hit a wall when running on the main thread. Frame drops, jank, and unresponsive UIs are the symptoms of pushing JavaScript beyond its comfort zone.

## WebAssembly to the Rescue

By compiling our rendering engine to WASM, we moved the heavy computation off the main thread entirely. The result: smooth 60fps rendering of complex fractal geometries.

## Benchmarks

Our WASM pipeline showed:
- 8x throughput improvement over pure JS
- Consistent 16ms frame times
- 90% reduction in main thread blocking
